Environmental issues surrounding food waste and how you can help

Food waste has become a big issue around the world. In New Zealand alone households throw away 157,389 tonnes of food waste each year, which equals out to about $1.17 billion each year. Most of our food waste comes from either cooking too much food and not eating the leftovers or from food going off in our fridges and freezers.

Our most Wasteful item is Bread with 29 million loaves of bread being thrown away in New Zealand each year. Bread goes mouldy faster than lots of other types of food as well as wasting the crusts as often we don't like to eat them

Food waste is a growing issue, but there are many things that can be done to reduce our food waste. Some very easy things to do to reduce food waste is to start at home compost to compose all those fruits and vegetables that are far past their use by date.
